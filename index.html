The key insights that can be gained is that even for very benign drugs such as Tylenol, there are many, many interactions. In this case, 1294 interactions are listed. Some key insights that can be made from this data are: Acetaminophen is involved in many drug interactions, both as a perpetrator and a victim. When combined with other drugs, it lead to potential toxicity, for instance: Can increase the serum concentration of some drugs: Abametapir and Abiraterone Can decrease the metabolism of some drugs: 8-chlorotheophylline and Acebutolol Can decrease the excretion rate of some drugs: Abacavir and Acrivastine Can increase the metabolism of Acetaminophen: Adalimumab Ademetionine can increase the hepatoprotective effects of Acetaminophen, which can be useful in preventing liver damage caused by an overdose of Acetaminophen. .
